## Purpose

Coastline v0.4 registers its **principal open problem** (Deferred Items; Anti-Claim #6): the transplant of Zurek's redundancy / stability / compressibility criteria from *configurational* records (the quantum-Darwinism setting) to *temporal* records has no worked exemplar. The literature synthesis (`docs/literature/synthesis-v0.1.md`, §3.1) names three missing ingredients precisely:

1. a **temporal pointer basis** (what is einselected for a temporal record?);
2. a definition of an **independent temporal-record fragment**;
3. a system in which the *same* elapsed time is **redundantly** recorded across many carriers.

This note supplies a toy instrument that makes all three explicit and computes Claim II's **redundancy** leg as a number. It does so by re-basing the configurational functional-information measure of quantum Darwinism (Riedel & Zurek 2010; Zurek 2009; and, operationally, *Functional Information in Quantum Darwinism*, arXiv:2509.17775, which defines `FI(δ) = log₂ R_δ`) onto a *temporal* pointer: a partial-information plot and a redundancy count `R_δ`.

What the note targets, concretely:

- A **definition** of a temporal-redundancy functional `R_δ[τ]` (§1).
- A **computation** distinguishing a *redundant* temporal record from an *anti-redundant* (entanglement-enhanced) one — making Coastline v0.4's "multipartite ≠ redundant" distinction numerical (§2; rigorous basis in Appendix B).
- A **mapping** onto the motional-squeezing / Sorci case, so the functional concretises Ledger CL-2026-006's reading (§3).
- A **principled reason** for the resolution-anchor / classification-anchor split the roadmap defers into the Claim IV measure-registration protocol (§4).
- An **honest account** of what the functional does *not* settle, including that it does **not** close the principal open problem (§6).

**v0.3 adds a numerical companion (§8):** the open-temporal-instrument exemplar (toolkit Module 3b) — the per-carrier distinguishability *derived* dynamically from a single-carrier master equation, and `R_δ(N, γ_φ t)` curves computed numerically, as the master-equation counterpart of §2's analytic poles, now matching the *configurational* computation of arXiv:2509.17775 on the temporal side. §2's analytics and §1.1's einselection assumption are **unchanged**; Anti-Claim #6 stays open.

## 1. The construction

### 1.1 Temporal pointer basis (ingredient i)

Fix a comparison protocol (Coastline v0.4 *Regime of Validity*) and a read-out epoch. Coarse-grain elapsed proper time into a finite **temporal pointer** `T` taking `d` values — the discrete labels of `d` resolvable elapsed-time bins, with phases `θ_j = 2π j / d` (`j = 0,…,d−1`) under a reference precession `ω`. The minimal non-trivial case is the **binary clock** `d = 2` (`T ∈ {early, late}`), used for all computations below; the generalisation to `d` bins is immediate.

**The load-bearing assumption.** This note assumes `T` is einselected by *some* mechanism and computes redundancy *conditional on* that assumption. The existence of such a mechanism for *temporal* records — a dynamically derived temporal pointer basis, the temporal analogue of environment-induced superselection — is the **hardest part** of the Coastline's principal open problem (Anti-Claim #6), and is **not** addressed here. The note's contribution is correspondingly precise but modest: it is an instrument that would read redundancy correctly *if* a temporal pointer were einselected, and it exhibits what those readings look like at the redundant and anti-redundant extremes. (A separate, lesser assumption — that `T` is *finite-dimensional* — is also deliberate and load-bearing for §4: redundancy is a statement about a *bounded* quantity of recorded information, `H_T ≤ log d`.)

### 1.2 Independent temporal-record fragment (ingredient ii)

A **carrier** is any degree of freedom whose read-out yields a (possibly noisy) estimate of `T` (Coastline Claim I "temporal record"). A set of `N` carriers is **fragmentable** if, conditioned on `T`, the carrier read-outs are statistically independent — i.e. each carrier is an *independent* witness of the same elapsed-time bin. A **fragment** `F` is a subset of the `N` carriers; we write `f = m/N` for the fraction in a fragment of `m` carriers.

Conditional independence given `T` is exactly what distinguishes a *redundant* record (independent compatible copies) from an *entanglement-enhanced* one (jointly read, anti-redundant). It is the temporal analogue of the disjoint-photon-fragment decomposition in Riedel & Zurek (2010).

*Caveat (flagged for a future version).* Conditional independence given `T` is natural when the carriers are physically distinct (separate ions, separate photon packets). For *multi-channel-within-a-single-subsystem* records — several observables of one constituent (Coastline v0.3 terminology) — the disjoint-fragment picture inherited from Riedel–Zurek is more questionable, since the "fragments" need not be independent or separately accessible. The computations below use the physically-distinct-carrier case; the single-subsystem multi-channel case is left open.

### 1.3 The functional (ingredient iii)

For a fragment of `m` carriers, define the **partial temporal information**

> `I(m) = I(T : F_m)`  — the mutual information between the temporal pointer `T` and the read-out of a fragment of `m` carriers.

A **redundant** temporal record is one whose partial-information plot `I(m)` rises to a **classical plateau** at `H_T` while `m ≪ N`: a small fragment already carries (almost) all the recordable elapsed-time information, and further carriers are redundant copies. The **temporal redundancy** at deficit `δ` is

> `R_δ[τ] = N / m_δ`,  where `m_δ = min { m : I(m) ≥ (1−δ) H_T }`.

`R_δ` is the *effective* number of disjoint fragments that each independently fix the elapsed-time bin to within deficit `δ` — the Riedel–Zurek `R_δ = 1/f_δ`, re-based on the temporal pointer of §1.1, and the redundancy whose logarithm is the functional information `FI(δ) = log₂ R_δ` (arXiv:2509.17775). It is a continuous ratio, not an integer tally: the literal count of disjoint fragments is `⌊N/m_δ⌋` (e.g. `⌊64/9⌋ = 7` for the §2 product record, whose ratio is `7.1`). A record whose plateau is reached only by the entire set (`m_δ = N`) has `R_δ = 1`: no redundancy. A record that never reaches `(1−δ)H_T` at all has `R_δ` **undefined** — a stronger failure, distinguished from `R_δ = 1` in §2.

## 2. Two analytic poles: redundant vs anti-redundant

We compute `I(m)` for the binary clock (`d = 2`, `H_T = 1` bit) in the two extreme configurations. In the **redundant** pole each carrier reads out a noisy copy of `T` with per-carrier flip probability `e ∈ (0, ½)` — the nuisance knob, standing for finite per-carrier distinguishability (dephasing, finite SNR). In the **anti-redundant** pole the global read-out is taken ideal (see below); `e` is therefore a property of the redundant pole only.

**Redundant pole (product record).** The `N` carriers are an unentangled product; each independently reads `T` with flip probability `e`. Conditioned on `T`, read-outs are i.i.d. — §1.2 holds maximally. This is the temporal analogue of Riedel & Zurek's scattered photons: independent carriers each imprinting the same bin.

**Anti-redundant pole (GHZ-shadow record).** The `N` carriers encode `T` so that only their *global* read-out carries it; every proper subset is marginally independent of `T`. The reference encoding is the cat state `(|0…0⟩ + (−1)^T |1…1⟩)/√2`, with the binary pointer stored in the relative phase (`0` or `π`): tracing out **any** carrier leaves the `T`-independent mixture `½|0…0⟩⟨0…0| + ½|1…1⟩⟨1…1|`, so every proper fragment carries *zero* elapsed-time information; only a coherent global (equatorial-parity) read-out of all `N` carriers recovers `T`. It is the canonical anti-redundant configuration — *many carriers, deliberately anti-redundant* (Coastline v0.4 Claim II terminology note; Claim V).

*Two cautions on this pole.* (i) The parity model used in the computation below is one explicit *classical* read-out channel. The anti-redundant result is in fact **stronger and measurement-independent**: tracing any proper fragment of the cat state leaves a `T`-*independent density operator*, so **no** measurement on that fragment — not just the parity read-out — can carry information about `T`. This follows from the Schmidt structure of the cat state; the derivation is **Appendix B**. The parity-bit accounting reproduces the right numbers but understates why they hold. (ii) The Heisenberg `F_τ ∝ N²` scaling often associated with such states belongs to the *continuous-phase* estimation problem of entanglement-enhanced metrology; it is invoked in §4 only as a non-load-bearing illustration of "anti-redundant ⇒ high *joint* sensitivity," not as a property of this binary-pointer information count. To isolate the structural statement we take the global read-out as **ideal**, so the whole record attains `H_T`; any global read-out imperfection only lowers that value and never lifts the proper-fragment information off zero.

| 1 | 0.016 | 0.2781 | 0.0000 |
| 2 | 0.031 | 0.4605 | 0.0000 |
| 4 | 0.062 | 0.6908 | 0.0000 |

The product record traces the characteristic concave rise to the `H_T = 1` bit plateau; `m_δ = 9` at `δ = 0.10`, so `R_{0.10} = 64/9 ≈ 7.1`. The GHZ-shadow record is a **step function**: `I(m) = 0` for every proper fragment, rising to `H_T = 1` bit only at `m = N` (ideal global read-out). Its redundancy follows from the *same* threshold definition — `m_δ = N`, hence `R_δ = N/N = 1`: the whole is the only fragment that records anything. (This is why the anti-redundant entry is `R_δ = 1`, not "undefined": unlike the noise-killed product rows below, the threshold *is* reached — but only by the entire set.)

This is the framework's "multipartite ≠ redundant" distinction as a number: the two configurations have the *same* `N` carriers and the GHZ pole carries *more* joint sensitivity in its continuous-phase realisation (§2 caution ii), yet `R_{0.10}` is `7.1` versus `1`.

**Redundancy vs per-carrier nuisance** (product pole, `N = 64`, `δ = 0.10`):

| per-carrier flip `e` | `m_δ` | `R_{0.10}` | `I(N)` (bits) |
| ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| 0.10 | 4 | 16.0 | 1.000 |
| 0.20 | 9 | 7.1 | 1.000 |
| 0.30 | 22 | 2.9 | 0.998 |
| 0.40 | — | — | 0.807 |
| 0.45 | — | — | 0.354 |

As per-carrier distinguishability degrades, the plateau retreats and `R_δ` collapses; by `e ≳ 0.40` no fragment of the whole `N = 64` reaches the `90%` threshold and `R_{0.10}` is undefined. **A redundant record can be destroyed by per-carrier nuisance alone, without any change in entanglement structure** — the observation §5 turns on.

## 3. Motional squeezing and the Sorci case

The Sorci et al. (2026) protocol (Ledger CL-2026-006) is the minimal anti-redundant case: a clock degree of freedom `C` coupled to a *single* squeezed motional mode `M` through the mass-defect Hamiltonian

> `Ĥ = Ĥ_c + ℏω(n̂ + ½) − (ℏω / 2mc²) Ĥ_c P̂²`,

producing a Ramsey-visibility loss `V ≃ 1 − (ε_m ω_c t)² sinh²(2r) / 16` (CL-2026-006). In the functional's terms:

- The reduced clock `C`, obtained by tracing out `M`, is the natural **fragment** of the two-carrier `{C, M}` record. Its retained temporal information as a stand-alone record is bounded by its coherence `V`.
- Squeezing `r` raises the clock–motion entanglement, so `V` **falls** monotonically with `sinh²(2r)`. The clock-as-fragment therefore loses elapsed-time information as `r` grows: the **stability** leg (Claim II.2) fails under partial trace exactly as CL-2026-006 records, and the redundancy of the reduced clock falls.
- Meanwhile the *joint* `{C, M}` Fisher information about elapsed time **rises** with `r` — that is the metrological point of squeezing.

So increasing `r` moves the Sorci protocol *toward the anti-redundant pole*: the reduced clock's redundancy falls while the joint `{C, M}` Fisher information rises. This is **structurally instructive** — the Sorci protocol *exemplifies the anti-redundant pole at the minimum-multipartite limit* (`N = 2`, "two carriers, not many"). But it is **not a test** of the functional, and supplies no quantitative observable: at `N = 2` the functional takes values in `{undefined, 1, 2}`, so "redundancy falls with `r`" is at most a **single transition** (from `2`, or undefined, to `1`) at some threshold `r`, not a curve. Turning the functional into a quantitative observable requires the many-carrier setting flagged in §6(c). What the Sorci case *does* establish is directional and structural: visibility loss is a redundancy/stability failure bought by anti-redundant entanglement, not a gain in consensus. A redundant trapped-ion temporal record would require the opposite design — *many independent, unentangled* carriers each reading the same elapsed-time bin (§2 product pole) — which squeezing-based protocols deliberately do not build.

## 4. Why the functional is the classification anchor and Fisher is the resolution anchor

The roadmap defers, into the Claim IV measure-registration protocol, a distinction between a **resolution anchor** (bounds what must be resolved) and a **classification anchor** (carries the structural content classified), and logs that conflating them was the CL-2026-007 v0.1 failure mode. This functional supplies a *principled* reason for the split, and the two anchors are about **different quantities**: Fisher information measures resolution of the *continuous* elapsed time `τ` (the underlying phase), while the redundancy functional measures how the *coarse bin* `T` is recorded. Within the toy itself this already forces the split — the load-bearing argument needs no metrology fact:

> Fisher information about continuous `τ` is **extensive** in the number of independent carriers (`F_τ(m) = Σ_{k≤m} F_k = m·F_1`, additive, **unbounded** as `N` grows), with no plateau. Mutual information about the coarse bin `T` is **bounded** by `H_T = log d`, and the fragment size at which it saturates is precisely the redundancy statement. The two are therefore structurally **non-substitutable**: one tracks resolution, the other redundancy.

Registering Fisher as a redundancy measure would invert this — reading an anti-redundant record as maximally redundant, the failure mode CL-2026-007 v0.1 fell into. The bounded-vs-extensive contrast is the structural reason the measure-registration protocol must register *one of each* and forbid substitution.

*(Separate illustration, not part of the argument.* The same anti-redundant multipartite structures are exactly those that achieve Heisenberg `F_τ ∝ N²` in the *continuous-phase* metrology problem — maximal *joint* sensitivity with `R_δ = 1` — which is why entanglement-enhanced clocks are anti-redundant by design. This dramatises the opposite-directions point but is not needed for it: the extensivity-vs-boundedness argument above holds within the binary-pointer regime alone.*)*

## 5. Bearing on the discriminant conditions (CL-2026-006 D1–D3)

The functional does **not**, by itself, discriminate structural consensus failure from nuisance — and seeing *why* sharpens the Ledger's discriminability gap. Two distinct causes lower `R_δ`:

- **Structural failure** (the witnessed effect): entanglement growth, tuned by squeezing `r` (and by the coupling `ε_m`), driving the record toward the anti-redundant pole.
- **Nuisance**: motional heating `ṅ`, dephasing `γ_φ`, preparation infidelity — all raise the per-carrier flip `e` and collapse the plateau (§2, second table), *with no change in entanglement structure*.

Both push `R_δ` down. The **value** of `R_δ` is therefore not a witness. What discriminates is its **controlled scaling**, which is exactly what CL-2026-006's discriminant conditions already demand, now expressed through the functional:

- **D3 (r-scan):** the structural contribution to the *information deficit* (via `V`) scales as `sinh²(2r)`; squeezing-preparation infidelity scales differently. Because `R_δ` is a *threshold* functional its own response is step-like, inheriting this `sinh²(2r)` dependence as a smooth curve only in the `N ≫ 2` limit; at the Sorci `N = 2` the observable is the structural *signature* (redundancy ↓ as joint Fisher ↑), not a fitted `R_δ(r)`. This discrimination is moreover **conditional**: it assumes a model of squeezing-preparation infidelity whose `r`-dependence is empirically distinguishable from `sinh²(2r)` (e.g. anti-squeezing-axis leakage versus common-mode dephasing scale differently). That model is inherited from CL-2026-006 D3 and is **not supplied here**; absent it, the `r`-scan separates structure from infidelity only if the two `r`-scalings genuinely differ.
- **D2 (null test):** the structural contribution tracks the coupling `ε_m` (lower trap frequency, heavier ion, lower clock frequency); the nuisance budget does not track `ε_m` the same way. `R_δ(ε_m)` in a null configuration bounds the nuisance floor that must be subtracted.
- **D1 (decomposition):** the per-carrier `e` is itself a sum of heating, dephasing, and detection contributions; the functional inherits whatever nuisance decomposition D1 supplies, and provides the redundancy observable that decomposition must explain.

In short: the functional concretises *where* the discrimination lives — in the controlled `r`- and `ε_m`-dependence, not in the bare value of `R_δ`. This is a candidate *measure-suggestive* sharpening of CL-2026-006's D2/D3; turning it into a measure-explicit `R_δ(r)` curve needs an `N ≫ 2` realisation (flagged for a future Ledger touch in §7).

## 6. What this note does and does not settle

**Does (modestly):**
- Gives the first *computed* partial-information plot and redundancy count `R_δ` re-based on a **temporal** pointer (with the three synthesis ingredients of §1 made explicit). The "first" is **corpus-relative and specific to the temporal re-basing**: the *configurational* functional-information computation is established and recent — including the operational `FI(δ) = log₂ R_δ` framework and its pure-dephasing `R_δ` simulations in arXiv:2509.17775 (not in this project's corpus; configurational). This note claims only that the *temporal* re-basing is new here, not priority against that literature.
- Renders "multipartite ≠ redundant" numerical (§2; rigorous in Appendix B) and supplies a structural reason for the resolution/classification-anchor split (§4).
- Maps both onto CL-2026-006 (§3) and onto its discriminant conditions (§5).

**Does not:**
- **It does not close the principal open problem, and does not touch Anti-Claim #6.** The product pole is a *candidate toy exemplar* of a redundant temporal record, not the demonstration the Coastline awaits. Four idealisations stand between it and that claim: (a) the temporal pointer is a coarse `d`-bin (here binary) label, not a continuous proper time; (b) the per-carrier read-out that turns each carrier's phase into a quasi-classical bit is *assumed* — and, more deeply, the temporal pointer is assumed *einselected* (§1.1), which is itself the hardest part of the open problem, not derived from any decoherence model; (c) the two poles are analytic endpoints — a non-toy instance would require a continuous-`τ`, dynamically einselected, many-carrier model. The *configurational* counterpart of exactly this — a heterogeneous pure-dephasing master-equation computation of `R_δ`, with the expected early-growth/plateau structure — already exists (arXiv:2509.17775); the open instrument is its **temporal** analogue, where the pointer is einselected elapsed-time (ingredient i) rather than a configurational pointer; (d) the anti-redundant pole is a *classical* parity model of the cat encoding `(|0…0⟩ + (−1)^T|1…1⟩)/√2`, whose measurement-independent justification is Appendix B but whose dynamical *origin* (why a cat, not a product) is not modelled. Whether any such instance counts as *the* worked exemplar that converts Claim II from analogy to demonstration is a **Coastline-level judgement reserved to the steward**; this note, by Lock-Key discipline, makes no such edit.
- It does not classify any external claim (that is a Ledger function) and does not commit a Claim IV canonical measure (the Coastline declines to, at v0.4).

## 8. Numerical companion: the open-temporal-instrument exemplar (Module 3b)

§6(c) flagged the *open temporal instrument* — the many-carrier, dynamically-einselected, continuous-`τ` extension — as the residual gap between §2's analytic poles and a non-toy exemplar. This section supplies the **many-carrier, dynamically-derived** part of that extension: a numerical companion to §2, computed by master-equation evolution (toolkit `tmc-numerics` Module 3b; the curves are committed at `numerics/results/open_instrument_redundancy_v0.1.json` and regenerated end-to-end by `numerics/examples/open_instrument_module.ipynb` — the §8 analogue of Appendix A's regeneration of §2). It does **not** supply the einselection: the load-bearing assumption of §1.1 is unchanged (§8.6).

### 8.1 The dynamical bridge — `ε` from a single-carrier master equation

In §2's product pole the per-carrier flip `e` was *posited*. Here it is *derived*. Each carrier encodes the binary pointer as a phase (`|+⟩` for `T=0`, `|−⟩` for `T=1`); pure dephasing `(γ_φ/2) D[σ_z]` over the read-out interval `t` degrades the distinguishability. The surviving phase-bin coherence is `c = e^{−γ_φ t}`, and the per-carrier distinguishability is

> `ε(γ_φ, t) = (1 − c)/2 = (1 − e^{−γ_φ t})/2`.

This is unambiguous: four standard measures **coincide**. The trace distance between the conditional carrier states is `D = c`, so the Helstrom minimum-error is `(1−D)/2 = ε`; a projective read-out in the (assumed) phase-bin basis flips with probability `ε`; and the Holevo information of the resulting ensemble is `χ = 1 − h₂(ε)` — the classical mutual information of that optimal read-out, i.e. the single-carrier `I(1)` of §2's channel. The distinguishability→flip map is a fact about the dephased states, not a modelling choice.

### 8.2 The `R_δ(N, γ_φ t)` curves and the undefined boundary

Feeding the derived `ε` into §2's product-pole channel and applying the §1.3 threshold gives `R_δ(N, γ_φ t)`. As `γ_φ t` grows, `ε → ½`, the partial-information plateau retreats, and `R_δ` collapses — §2's "a redundant record destroyed by per-carrier nuisance alone" now exhibited *dynamically*, as a function of physical dephasing time. The collapse has an explicit **undefined boundary**: `R_δ` is undefined once even the full set fails `I(N) ≥ (1−δ)H_T`, i.e. `ε ≥ ε_crit(N, δ)`. The first sweep point at which `R_δ` becomes undefined **rises with `N`** — more independent carriers tolerate more per-carrier nuisance. (The true threshold `ε_crit(N, δ)` / `γ_φ t_crit` is a *continuous* quantity, where `I(N) = (1−δ)H_T` exactly; the table below reports the **first grid point of the committed sweep** (`γ_φ t` on a 12-point grid over `[0.1, 2.0]`) at which the plateau is no longer reached, so the values are grid-resolution-dependent lower-resolution proxies, not the exact thresholds.)

| first undefined `γ_φ t` in sweep (δ = 0.10) | 0.27 | 0.62 | 0.79 | 1.14 | 1.48 | 1.83 |

Below the boundary `R_δ` is a finite redundancy count; at and above it the record never reaches the `(1−δ)H_T` plateau — the *undefined* failure of §2, there distinguished from `R_δ = 1`.

### 8.4 The structural point — redundancy lost without entanglement

from math import comb, log2

def h2(p):
    if p <= 0 or p >= 1: return 0.0
    return -p*log2(p) - (1-p)*log2(1-p)

def I_product(m, e):
    """Mutual information I(T;F_m) in bits, binary pointer, m i.i.d. noisy copies."""
    H_T_given_F = 0.0
    for k in range(m+1):                       # k = number of carriers reading '1'
        p0 = comb(m, k) * e**k * (1-e)**(m-k)  # P(k | T=0)
        p1 = comb(m, k) * (1-e)**k * e**(m-k)  # P(k | T=1)
        pk = 0.5*p0 + 0.5*p1
        if pk > 0:
            H_T_given_F += pk * h2(0.5*p1/pk)
    return 1.0 - H_T_given_F                    # H_T = 1 bit

def I_ghz(m, N):                                # global-parity (GHZ-shadow) record;
    return 1.0 if m == N else 0.0               # ideal global read-out: I(N) = H_T

def redundancy(N, e, delta):
    for m in range(1, N+1):
        if I_product(m, e) >= (1-delta):
            return N/m, m
    return None, None

if __name__ == "__main__":
    N = 64

        R, m = redundancy(N, e, 0.10)

## Appendix B — Why every proper fragment carries zero (Schmidt argument)

The anti-redundant result of §2 is **measurement-independent** and follows from the structure of the cat state, not from the choice of parity read-out. Encode the binary pointer as

> `|ψ_T⟩ = (|0⟩^{⊗N} + (−1)^T |1⟩^{⊗N}) / √2`,  `T ∈ {0, 1}`.

Partition the `N` carriers into a fragment `F` of `m` carriers and its complement `E` of `N − m` carriers, with `1 ≤ m ≤ N − 1` (a *proper* fragment). Writing `|0⟩^{⊗N} = |0⟩_F |0⟩_E` and `|1⟩^{⊗N} = |1⟩_F |1⟩_E`, the reduced state on `F` is

> `ρ_F^{(T)} = Tr_E |ψ_T⟩⟨ψ_T| = ½(|0⟩_F⟨0| + |1⟩_F⟨1|) + ½(−1)^T ( |0⟩_F⟨1|·⟨1|0⟩_E + |1⟩_F⟨0|·⟨0|1⟩_E ).`

Because `⟨0|1⟩_E = ⟨0|1⟩^{N−m} = 0` whenever `N − m ≥ 1`, both cross terms vanish:

> `ρ_F^{(T)} = ½(|0⟩_F⟨0| + |1⟩_F⟨1|)`,  **independent of `T`.**

Since `ρ_F^{(0)} = ρ_F^{(1)}` as density operators, for *any* POVM `{M_x}` on `F` the outcome statistics `p(x | T) = Tr(M_x ρ_F^{(T)})` are independent of `T`. Hence `I(F_m : T) = 0` for every proper fragment and **every** measurement — not merely for the parity read-out of Appendix A. For the whole set (`m = N`), `⟨ψ_0 | ψ_1⟩ = ½(1 − 1) = 0`, so the two pointer states are orthogonal and perfectly distinguishable by a global measurement, giving `I = H_T` under an ideal read-out.

This is the rigorous basis for the §2 GHZ-shadow column and for `R_δ = 1`. The contrast with the redundant pole is now transparent: there each carrier *individually* has `T`-dependent statistics, so information is available in every fragment; here the `T`-dependence lives only in a global coherence that no proper fragment can see.
