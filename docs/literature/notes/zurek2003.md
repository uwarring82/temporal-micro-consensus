## Summary

The foundational review of the decoherence-and-einselection program for the quantum-to-classical transition. It argues that environmental monitoring continually selects a small set of robust "pointer states" (einselection) out of the vast Hilbert space, suppressing macroscopic superpositions and yielding effective classicality. The final sections (§VII onward) pivot from decoherence-as-censorship to the constructive "environment as a witness" view: classical objectivity arises because pointer-state information is recorded *redundantly* across many environment fragments, so many observers can independently agree on a system's state without disturbing it. This is the seed of quantum Darwinism.

## Key points

- Pointer states are picked out by a predictability sieve (least entropy production under environmental monitoring); they are the "fittest" survivors and the basis the environment redundantly copies.
- Objectivity is defined operationally as the ability of many observers to reach consensus from disjoint environment fragments — quantified by the redundancy ratio R (roughly the number of independent copies of the system's information).
- Introduces redundancy-based measures: basis-independent mutual-information redundancy R_I and a basis-dependent, locally-accessible R_J; preferred basis = the one maximizing redundancy; also a redundancy *rate* Ṙ as a "fitness" measure.
- Action distance (total action to erase a record in the environment) is a metric distinguishing robust, multiply-copied pointer records from fragile, hard-to-read superposition records (illustrated by the spin "bit-by-byte" model).
- Envariance (environment-assisted invariance) is offered as a route to ignorance, Born's rule, and the reduced density matrix without circular appeal to probabilities.

## Relevance to the framework

This is the canonical statement of the redundancy/consensus architecture (Claim II, Claim V) that the framework borrows wholesale and re-applies to *temporal* rather than configurational records. The redundancy ratio, mutual-information and action-distance measures map directly onto the framework's operational anchors (Claim IV: redundancy of temporal records, robustness under coarse-graining). The pointer-state-as-survivor logic underwrites the idea that an emergent classical variable (here position; in the framework, proper time) is whatever the environment copies most robustly.
