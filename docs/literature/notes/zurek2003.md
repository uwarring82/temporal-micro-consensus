## Core claim
Environmental monitoring continually einselects a small set of robust pointer states out of Hilbert space, suppressing macroscopic superpositions and yielding effective classicality; classical objectivity then arises because pointer-state information is recorded *redundantly* across many environment fragments, so many observers independently agree on a system's state without disturbing it ("environment as a witness").

## Method
Decoherence-and-einselection program. Pointer states are selected by a predictability sieve (least entropy production / most robust under monitoring). The constructive turn (§VII, "Environment as a witness") quantifies objectivity via redundancy: a redundancy ratio R (number of independent copies of the system's information), a basis-independent mutual-information redundancy R_I and a basis-dependent locally-accessible R_J, a redundancy rate Ṙ as a "fitness" measure, and an *action distance* metric (total action to erase a record) distinguishing robust multiply-copied records from fragile ones. Envariance (environment-assisted invariance) is offered as a non-circular route to ignorance and Born's rule.

## Relation to the five Coastline claims
- **Claim I — emergent proper time / temporal record:** Supports by analogy. The whole program establishes that a classical variable (here position/pointer observable) is an *emergent effective* quantity defined by what the environment records, not a fundamental microscopic one. Transplanting "record" from configurational to temporal makes the environment-fragment imprint the prototype of a "temporal record." The paper itself is about configurational records and is silent on time per se.
- **Claim II — redundancy / stability / compressibility:** Supports (canonical source). This is the originating statement of the redundancy/stability architecture the framework borrows: pointer states are *stable* (einselected survivors), their information is *redundant* (many independent copies R), and reading a small fragment suffices (*compressible* — only a fraction of the environment is needed for consensus). The framework re-applies all three legs to temporal inference.
- **Claim III — failure mode / nuisance-discrimination:** Supports. The predictability sieve and action-distance metric provide the discrimination logic: fragile, hard-to-read, low-action-distance records (superpositions) fail to support objective consensus, whereas robust multiply-copied records do. This is the configurational analogue of Claim III's demand to separate structural failure (no consensus variable) from generic decoherence.
- **Claim IV — operational anchors (Fisher / mutual info / cross-probe / coarse-graining):** Strong anchor for mutual-information and coarse-graining. Redundancy R and the mutual-information redundancy R_I map onto "mutual information between records"; the basis-maximizing-redundancy criterion and fragment-size analysis are the robustness-under-coarse-graining anchor. No Fisher information explicitly; cross-probe mismatch implicit in inter-fragment agreement.
- **Claim V — positioning (PW bipartite vs quantum-Darwinism configurational vs multipartite redundancy):** Supports as the *configurational* multipartite-redundancy pole. This is the quantum-Darwinism architecture applied to configuration; the framework's novelty is to keep the multipartite-redundancy machinery but switch the recorded quantity from configuration to elapsed time, contrasting both with the PW bipartite-correlation pole.
