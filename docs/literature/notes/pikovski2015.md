## Core claim
Gravitational time dilation generically decoheres the centre-of-mass (COM) of any composite quantum system with internal DOF, with no external environment required. Because gravity couples to total mass-energy, the internal Hamiltonian couples to COM position via H_int = Φ(x)H₀/c², so a spatial superposition at different potentials accumulates a proper-time difference that correlates internal and COM states and washes out COM coherence — and even Earth's weak time dilation suffices to decohere gram-scale objects (τ_dec ≈ 10⁻³ s for N ~ 10²³, Δx = 1 µm).

## Method
Composite system with N internal harmonic modes in thermal equilibrium at temperature T; tracing out the internal "clock/environment" suppresses COM off-diagonals. COM interference visibility V(t) = |⟨e^{−iH₀Δτ/ℏ}⟩| (Eq. 4) depends only on accumulated proper-time difference Δτ — manifestly coordinate-invariant. A derived master equation (Eqs. 5–6) gives Gaussian (non-Markovian) decay with rate set by internal energy fluctuations ΔE₀² = ⟨H₀²⟩−⟨H₀⟩² = C_v k_B T² = N(k_B T)² (high-T); decoherence time τ_dec = √(2/N)·ℏc²/(k_B T g Δx) (Eq. 3). Effective total mass m_tot = m + Ē₀/c²; pointer basis driven toward position/momentum. No free model parameters; no modification of QM or GR.

## Relation to the five Coastline claims
- **Claim I — emergent proper time / temporal record:** Strongly supports. The internal DOF *are* the temporal record — an intrinsic clock whose accumulated Δτ is the elapsed-evolution information — and classicality of the COM emerges precisely from this record, not from any external time variable. This is the clearest physics precedent for Claim I's stance that proper time is carried by ordinary DOF and that classicality is a downstream consequence.
- **Claim II — redundancy / stability / compressibility:** Supports the stability leg, silent on redundancy and compressibility. The N internal modes act as an *intrinsic environment* and the COM is driven to a stable classical (position/momentum) pointer basis — a stability statement. But there is no fragment/redundancy analysis (the internal bath is traced as a single environment, not partitioned into many independently-readable copies) and no compressibility metric. So it supplies the decoherence physics underlying Claim II's "temporal record" but not the multipartite redundancy statistics; an observer cannot read Δτ from disjoint fragments here — strictly, this is record *destruction* of COM coherence, not record *proliferation*.
- **Claim III — failure mode / nuisance-discrimination:** Supports the discrimination strongly. The mechanism is explicitly distinguished from (i) external scattering/collisional decoherence — it scales *linearly* in Δx (vs quadratic) and persists for fully isolated systems — and (ii) spontaneous-collapse models (Penrose, Diósi, GRW/CSL) which require modifying QM/GR; here there are no free parameters. This is exactly Claim III's structural-failure-vs-generic-nuisance distinction: a time-specific, universal decoherence channel that a careful analysis must separate from ordinary environmental nuisance. The in-principle reversibility ("revivals" at large Δτ, with exponentially long recurrence times) is a clean marker that this is structural, not thermal-irreversible, decoherence.
- **Claim IV — operational anchors (Fisher / mutual info / cross-probe / coarse-graining):** Supports Fisher-type and robustness anchors; silent on mutual-info-between-records and explicit cross-probe mismatch. V(t) set by Δτ (Eq. 4) and the ΔE₀²-driven Gaussian rate are operational handles, with ΔE₀² (the energy-fluctuation / heat-capacity moment) the Fisher-information-like time-energy resource (more internal energy spread → faster, sharper temporal record). Robustness under system size (N) and coarse heat-capacity estimation is discussed (Fig. 3 parameter map vs thermal-emission decoherence). No multi-fragment mutual information; cross-probe consensus not formalized.
- **Claim V — positioning (PW bipartite vs quantum-Darwinism configurational vs multipartite redundancy):** Sits at the *bipartite* pole (internal clock vs COM), in the relativistic Page–Wootters-adjacent spirit rather than multipartite redundancy. It is the dynamical, GR-grounded version of "internal clock decoheres a system," not a many-fragment redundancy account. It thus marks the same gap as [[zych2011]]: the framework's Claim V must promote this single intrinsic-clock record to a redundant, multiply-witnessed temporal consensus.

## Open questions / flags
Title, authors, journal, DOI confirmed; PDF is the full 7-page article including Methods (prior note said 5 pp.; the supplied file contains the Methods Hamiltonian derivation and master-equation appendix). The high-T harmonic model for ΔE₀² is acknowledged crude for composition estimates and expected to break down at low T. The decoherence here destroys COM coherence by tracing an *inaccessible* intrinsic environment — it is not yet a proliferation/consensus picture, so it constrains Claim II/V only as the underlying mechanism. Open question: whether the accumulated-Δτ record can be made *redundantly readable* (multipartite) rather than merely a coherence sink, which is what the framework requires.
