## Summary
The paper shows that gravitational time dilation generically decoheres the centre-of-mass (COM) of any composite quantum system that has internal degrees of freedom. Because gravity couples to total mass-energy, the internal Hamiltonian H₀ couples to COM position via H_int = Φ(x) H₀/c², so a spatial superposition at different gravitational potentials accumulates a proper-time difference that correlates internal and COM states and washes out COM coherence — no external environment required. Even Earth's weak time dilation is shown to suffice to decohere micron-to-gram-scale objects.

## Key points
- The internal DOF act as an *intrinsic clock/environment*: tracing them out suppresses COM off-diagonal elements; visibility V = |⟨e^{−iH₀Δτ/ℏ}⟩| depends only on accumulated proper-time difference Δτ (Eq. 4) — manifestly coordinate-invariant.
- Decoherence time τ_dec = √(2/N) ℏc²/(k_B T g Δx) (Eq. 3): scales *linearly* in superposition size Δx (unlike quadratic for collisional decoherence) and improves with particle number N and temperature T (more internal energy fluctuation ΔE₀² = C_v k_B T²).
- A derived master equation (Eqs. 5–6) gives Gaussian (non-Markovian) decay; the gravitational potential renormalizes an effective total mass m_tot = m + Ē₀/c², and the pointer basis is driven toward classical position/momentum variables.
- Effect is reversible in principle ("revivals" at large accumulated Δτ) but recurrence times grow exponentially with system size, so it is effectively irreversible "for all practical purposes."
- No free model parameters and no modification of QM or GR — explicitly contrasted with spontaneous-collapse models (Penrose, Diósi, GRW/CSL); a numerical estimate gives τ_dec ≈ 10⁻³ s for a gram-scale (N ~ 10²³), Δx = 1 µm superposition.
