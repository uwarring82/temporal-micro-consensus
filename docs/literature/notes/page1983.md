## Core claim
For a closed system constrained to a stationary global state (energy superselection, as for the Universe), apparent dynamics is fully recovered from stationary observables: time is an internal clock reading, and evolution appears as conditional correlations between a chosen clock subsystem and the rest, with no reference to an external time parameter.

## Method
Relational / conditional-probability formalism. A bipartite split H = H_c + H_r (clock plus rest, non-interacting) is taken; time-averaged ("smeared") projectors P̄_τ onto clock readings are stationary and thus legitimate observables. The conditional expectation E(A|τ) = tr(P̄_τ A P̄_τ ρ)/tr(P̄_τ ρ) is shown to reproduce Heisenberg evolution of the rest of the system. Illustrated by a toy model of n spin-j particles precessing about z, with one particle's spin direction read as the clock.

## Relation to the five Coastline claims
- **Claim I — emergent proper time / temporal record:** Supports (conceptual root). Time here is not the expectation of a microscopic time operator but an inferred correlation: the clock subsystem's reading is a "temporal record" in the framework's sense, and elapsed evolution is read off conditionally. This is the originating statement that proper time is relational/inferential rather than a fundamental external parameter.
- **Claim II — redundancy / stability / compressibility:** Silent. The construction is single-clock and contains no notion of multiple independent records, redundant proliferation, or compressibility. Stability of the smeared projectors as stationary observables is asserted, but not in a consensus/many-records sense.
- **Claim III — failure mode / nuisance-discrimination:** Weakly relevant. The remark that resolving fine time intervals requires large energy exchange (a time–energy tradeoff, citing DeWitt-type bounds) prefigures a precision/failure regime, but the paper does not develop a structural-failure-vs-decoherence discrimination; treat as silent on the framework's specific failure mode.
- **Claim IV — operational anchors (Fisher / mutual info / cross-probe / coarse-graining):** Partly supports the coarse-graining anchor: the time-smearing of projectors is an explicit coarse-graining of the clock that is required for the observables to be well-defined and stationary. No Fisher information, no mutual-information-between-records, no cross-probe mismatch (single clock only).
- **Claim V — positioning (PW bipartite vs quantum-Darwinism configurational vs multipartite redundancy):** Supports as the canonical contrast point. This is *the* bipartite clock–system correlation paradigm. In the framework's positioning it is one available channel (a single witness), not the consensus itself; the Coastline framework generalizes from this bipartite correlation to multipartite redundancy of temporal records.
