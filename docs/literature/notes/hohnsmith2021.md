## Summary
Establishes a rigorous equivalence ("the trinity") between three approaches to relational quantum dynamics: (1) gauge-invariant relational Dirac observables in the clock-neutral Dirac-quantized picture, (2) the Page–Wootters conditional-probability / Schrödinger picture, and (3) the relational Heisenberg picture from quantum symmetry reduction. Using quantum reduction maps and a covariant-POVM quantization of clock time observables, the authors show all three are faces of the same relational theory and resolve several long-standing criticisms of the Page–Wootters formalism.

## Key points
- Clock time is quantized as a *covariant POVM* (not a self-adjoint operator canonically conjugate to H_C), via a G-twirl of kinematical observables; this resolves the Unruh–Wald nonmonotonicity objection — the POVM clock is monotonic for bounded Hamiltonians and admits a consistent probability interpretation.
- Resolves Kuchař's three criticisms: (i) PaW conditional probabilities are gauge-invariant relational-observable expectation values, not constraint-violating; (ii) gives correct propagators / transition probabilities without auxiliary ancillas, ideal clocks, or approximations; (iii) shows the claimed normalization ambiguity does not arise (PaW inner product = physical inner product by group averaging).
- Clarifies that clock–system entanglement in the PaW formalism is *kinematical*; the same relational dynamics can arise without it.
- Constructs temporal-frame-change maps (changing which subsystem is the clock) through the clock-neutral picture, and exhibits a clock-dependent *temporal nonlocality* effect when a clock is in a superposition of reading different times (a "self-reference" phenomenon).
- Conclusion flags the open "global problem of time": interactions generically make clocks nonmonotonic (turning points), yielding modified/non-unitary Schrödinger dynamics — clock pathologies may need navigation by patching different clock choices.

## Relevance to the framework
Foundational for **Claim V** positioning and for the conceptual hygiene of the whole program: it pins down exactly what a relational temporal record is (gauge-invariant Dirac observable / covariant clock POVM) and unifies the bipartite Page–Wootters lineage with Dirac and Heisenberg pictures. The covariant-POVM treatment supports **Claim I** (proper time as an emergent, non-sharp observable). Its temporal-nonlocality and turning-point/non-monotonicity discussion is directly relevant to **Claim III** failure modes (when a clock ceases to support a usable monotone temporal variable). The temporal-frame-change construction (which subsystem reads time) is suggestive for the **Claim V** multipartite extension, though this paper itself remains bipartite/few-clock.
