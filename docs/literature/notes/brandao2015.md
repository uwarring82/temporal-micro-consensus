## Summary

Proves that one pillar of quantum Darwinism — objectivity of observables — is a *generic* consequence of quantum theory rather than a model-specific accident. Whenever a finite-dimensional system distributes information into many initially-uncorrelated environment fragments via any quantum channel, observers reading individual fragments can effectively access at most *classical* information about one and the same system observable. The result needs no assumed system/environment split or symmetry, so any subsystem is "objectively measured" by the rest, and it yields a clean operational characterization of quantum discord as a by-product.

## Key points

- Theorem 1: for a CPTP channel Λ: A → B₁⊗…⊗Bₙ from a d_A-dimensional system to n fragments, for fixed δ there is a measurement (POVM) and a fraction (1−δ) of fragments whose effective dynamics are ε-close (diamond norm) to a fixed measure-and-prepare channel with the *same* POVM — bound scales ~ (log²d_A / n)^{1/3}, vanishing as n grows.
- The shared POVM is interpretable as the pointer observable; the result is "symmetric" — no privileged system/environment cut, any subsystem objectively measured by the others.
- Splits emergent objectivity into *objectivity of observables* (proven generic here, rooted in monogamy of entanglement / no-cloning) and *objectivity of outcomes* (NOT generic — depends on the specific interaction; Theorem 2 / Proposition 3 give partial conditions).
- Derives an operational interpretation of quantum discord as the asymptotic minimum average loss of correlations when one party redistributes its share to many others (Corollary 4).
- Distinguishes its assumption-light "final-state" approach from spectrum-broadcast-structure / dynamics-based derivations.

## Relevance to the framework

This is the strongest "genericity" result for the configurational case and is load-bearing for Claim II/Claim V: it says multipartite redundancy *forces* classicality of a shared observable under almost any dynamics, which is exactly the universality the framework wants when it argues a classical proper-time variable emerges generically from redundant temporal records. The crisp split between objectivity-of-observables (generic) and objectivity-of-outcomes (model-dependent) is a precise tool for Claim III: it tells the framework which aspects of a temporal-consensus variable should hold structurally vs which can fail as model-specific nuisance/decoherence.
