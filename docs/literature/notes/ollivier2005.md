## Summary

The long companion paper to ollivier2004, developing the environment-as-witness program in full and illustrating it on the standard photon-scattering / controlled-shift decoherence model. It sets up a rigorous information-theoretic framework for the redundant imprinting of system observables in environment fragments, proves that the only redundantly broadcast observables are the familiar einselected pointer observables, and discusses at length the assumptions (initial product state, preferred recorded observable, non-evolving environment) under which objectivity emerges.

## Key points

- Reaffirms the operational definition of objectivity (accessible to many, no prior knowledge, no prior agreement) and shows redundant imprinting is the robust, selective criterion separating objective from merely complete information.
- Spells out four working assumptions and analyses how relaxing each (mixed environment, weak/strong self-Hamiltonian H_S, interacting subenvironments, evolving records) degrades or modifies objectivity — a careful failure-mode catalogue.
- Mixed/coarse-grained environments can give selective amnesia: the predictability-sieve observable need not coincide with the observable easiest to read from a noisy E, so an observer may recover only a coarse-grained pointer.
- Explicitly raises (§VII) the prospect of *objective histories* — "sequences of time-ordered redundant records" / approximate instantaneous pointer states under frequent broadband monitoring — and links them to consistent-histories formalism.
- Quantum Darwinism requires *stronger* assumptions than bare decoherence (it is harder to use a system as a communication channel than as a disposal); existence of pointer states is necessary but not sufficient for redundant imprinting.

## Relevance to the framework

The §VII discussion of *time-ordered redundant records* and objective histories is the closest configurational precursor to the framework's whole premise: a classical temporal-consensus variable as a sequence of redundant records of elapsed evolution. The assumption-relaxation catalogue (esp. mixed environments, selective amnesia, evolving records) is a direct resource for Claim III — distinguishing structural failure of the temporal-consensus variable from generic decoherence/nuisance. Redundancy and channel-capacity arguments feed Claim IV.
