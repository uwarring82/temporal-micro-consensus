## Summary

This Letter argues that several familiar systematic frequency shifts in atomic clocks — the gravitational redshift and motion-induced shifts (second-order/quadratic Doppler, micromotion) — can be reinterpreted as direct consequences of the *mass defect* in quantum atomic physics, i.e. the fact that the excited and ground states have slightly different rest masses (M_e = M_g + ħω₀/c²). Crucially, this reading does not invoke the relativistic notion of "proper time" (time dilation) of the atom at all; it follows from E = Mc² plus standard non-relativistic quantum mechanics with mass-corrected Hamiltonians. The authors further predict previously-unconsidered field-induced shifts for trapped ions arising from the mass defect.

## Key points

- Mass defect: the ground/excited internal energies are E_j^(0) = M_j c², so the transition frequency in a gravitational potential φ acquires ω = ω₀(1 + φ/c²) directly from the potential energy M_j φ — reproducing the GR redshift to leading order *without* time-dilation arguments (called a "quasi-classical" route).
- For free atoms, exact quantum-relativistic absorption/emission formulas are derived from energy-momentum conservation; they differ from the textbook Lorentz/Doppler formula because the recoil shift and the unequal velocities v_g ≠ v_e (due to the mass defect) matter — there is no single inertial frame for the moving atom.
- The authors argue a spatially localized "proper time of a quantum particle" in a confining potential is conceptually unclear/contradictory, and that the mass-defect (Hamiltonian/eigenvalue) approach is internally consistent without needing it; even the velocity operator v̂ has no single definition because v depends on which mass M_j.
- For trapped ions, the mass defect modifies the vibrational structure differently for |g⟩ and |e⟩ (ω_αα ≠ ω₀), giving the quadratic-Doppler and micromotion shifts; predicts new residual quadrupole, dipole-cloud, and field-induced shifts at the 10⁻¹⁸–10⁻¹⁹ level relevant to next-generation clocks.
- Predecessor / lower-rigor companion to martinezlahuerta2022, which later supplied the fully ab initio Hamiltonian derivation.

## Relevance to the framework

Strongly relevant to Claim I and Claim V positioning. It is a concrete physics argument that the standard "proper time" story is *not* microscopically fundamental for an atomic clock — the observable shifts are reproduced by energy/mass bookkeeping of internal records, and the authors explicitly question whether a sharply localized proper time of a quantum particle is even well-defined. That is essentially the framework's Claim I (proper time emergent, not microscopically sharp), arrived at from clock physics. The state-dependent tick rate (ω_αα depending on motional state, position, fields) is exactly the kind of per-record temporal-rate variation the framework's cross-probe-mismatch / robustness anchors (Claim IV) would quantify across redundant records.
