## Summary

Sharpens quantum Darwinism by deriving objectivity at the level of quantum *states* rather than only information-theoretic conditions, using the spectrum-broadcast-structure (SBS) idea. For the standard illuminated-dielectric-sphere model the authors prove that, even when the photonic environment is initially noisy (not thermal), the time-asymptotic system–environment state still converges to a SBS — a classically-correlated state in which each accessible macrofraction of the environment redundantly encodes the same pointer-state information — so the object's location becomes objective in a model- and dynamics-robust way.

## Key points

- Spectrum broadcast structure: ϱ_{S:fE}(∞) = Σ_i p_i |x̄_i⟩⟨x̄_i| ⊗ ϱ_i^{E1} ⊗ … with the environmental states ϱ_i having *mutually orthogonal supports* — a stronger, state-level certificate of objectivity than mutual-information conditions alone.
- Introduces "macrofractions" (coarse-graining the environment into observer-accessible chunks scaling with total photon number); requires both decoherence (off-diagonal trace-norm → 0) *and* perfect distinguishability of the conditional macrostates (state fidelity → 0).
- Proves SBS emergence survives initial environmental *noise* — robustness/generic character of objectivity beyond pure thermal models.
- Recovers the quantum-Darwinism mutual-information plateau I → H_S as a *consequence* of SBS (so SBS ⟹ entropic objectivity), but not conversely.
- "Singular points" of decoherence (Perron–Frobenius fixed spectrum) survive scattering and can be exploited to faithfully broadcast a classical message through an arbitrarily noisy environment.

## Relevance to the framework

SBS gives a *state-structure* criterion for objectivity that is stricter than the mutual-information plateau — directly useful for Claim III, where one must discriminate genuine breakdown of the consensus variable from generic decoherence: SBS demands both decoherence AND macrostate distinguishability, so failure of the latter is a clean structural-failure signature. The macrofraction coarse-graining and orthogonal-support requirement map onto Claim IV's robustness-under-coarse-graining and cross-probe-mismatch anchors, and onto the redundancy/compressibility content of Claim II — now phrased for a candidate temporal-record state.
