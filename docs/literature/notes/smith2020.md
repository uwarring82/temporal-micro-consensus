## Summary
Extends the Page–Wootters formalism to relativistic particles carrying an internal "clock" degree of freedom moving through curved/Minkowski spacetime, and constructs the conditional probability that one clock reads proper time τ_A given a second clock reads τ_B. When the clocks' centre-of-mass states are localized in momentum, the conditional probabilities reproduce ordinary (classical) special-relativistic time dilation; when one clock is in a *superposition* of localized momentum wave packets, a genuinely quantum correction to the observed time dilation appears.

## Key points
- Proper time is treated as a covariant POVM T_clock (effect operators E_clock(τ)), not a self-adjoint operator; mass M_clock = m + H_clock/c² is treated as a self-adjoint observable.
- Helstrom–Holevo bound yields a time–energy/mass uncertainty relation: ΔM_clock ΔT_clock ≥ 1/(2c²); optimal covariant clocks saturate the Mandelstam–Tamm and Cramér–Rao bounds, with Fisher information F[τ;ρ(τ)] = 4⟨(ΔH_clock)²⟩.
- Classical regime (Gaussian momentum packets): τ_A = (γ_B/γ_A)τ_B to leading relativistic order — matches special relativity.
- Quantum time dilation: for a momentum superposition, ⟨T_A⟩ = (γ_C⁻¹ + γ_Q⁻¹)τ_B, with γ_Q⁻¹ the nonclassical correction (Eq. 23–25); vanishes when the two packet momenta coincide.
- Experimental estimate: for ⁸⁷Rb clocks (v ≈ 5 and 15 m/s branches), γ_Q⁻¹ ≈ 10⁻¹⁵, requiring ~10 s coherence at 10⁻¹⁴ s clock resolution — comparable to existing atom-interferometer coherence times.

## Relevance to the framework
Strong **Claim IV** anchor: makes Fisher information about elapsed proper time and the time–energy uncertainty relation explicit, and gives a concrete *cross-probe mismatch* (two clocks reading different proper times). Bears on **Claim I** (proper time as an emergent, POVM-encoded record rather than a sharp microscopic variable) and **Claim III** (the quantum correction is the regime where a single classical time-dilation value no longer suffices). A bridge between the bipartite Page–Wootters lineage (**Claim V**) and operational clock metrology.
