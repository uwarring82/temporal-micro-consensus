## Summary

Derives, from first principles, the passive gravitational mass of a generic *bound (composite)* system in an external gravitational field, and shows that — contrary to a long line of earlier results reporting a "virial anomaly" (gravity coupling to `R + 3T + 2U` rather than to the total energy) — gravity couples only to the **total internal energy** `E = mc²` of the bound system, exactly as the equivalence principle requires. The apparent anomaly is a coordinate artefact that disappears once the internal degrees of freedom are described in the correct local (centre-of-momentum, rest-frame) coordinates. The composite system on a curved background then takes a **single-particle form** whose effective mass is its rest-frame internal energy `H_rest/c²`.

## Key points

- Method: write the N-particle Lagrangian in two coordinate sets — an arbitrary worldline `Q^μ(t)` and the CM/center-of-momentum primed frame — valid when the metric is approximately constant across the system (conditions Eq. 3), so tidal effects are negligible and a covariant CM exists.
- Legendre transform yields the total Hamiltonian `H_N = √(−g₀₀(c²P_iP^i + H²_rest))` (Eq. 15): a composite system on a narrow world-tube behaves as a single particle of mass `H_rest/c²`.
- The internal energy `H_rest` (e.g. an internal clock energy `H_c`) therefore enters the mass that couples to motion and gravity — this is the mass-energy/proper-time coupling underlying time-dilation effects in clocks.
- Worked examples (hydrogenlike two-charge system on a post-Newtonian metric, etc.) show the "virial" couplings of prior work are removed by the correct rest-frame coordinates.
