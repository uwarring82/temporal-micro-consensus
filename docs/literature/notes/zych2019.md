## Core claim
The passive gravitational mass of a bound composite system couples to its *total* internal energy `E = mc²`, exactly as the equivalence principle requires — not to the `R + 3T + 2U` "virial anomaly" reported by earlier work, which is shown to be a coordinate artefact. In correct local rest-frame coordinates the composite reduces to a single-particle form with effective mass `H_rest/c²`.

## Method
First-principles derivation from the N-particle Lagrangian written in two coordinate sets: an arbitrary worldline `Q^μ(t)` and the center-of-momentum (rest-frame) primed frame. The construction is valid when the metric is approximately constant across the system (conditions Eq. 3), so tidal effects are negligible and a covariant CM exists. A Legendre transform yields `H_N = √(−g₀₀(c²P_iP^i + H²_rest))` (Eq. 15) — a composite on a narrow world-tube behaving as one particle of mass `H_rest/c²`. The internal energy (e.g. an internal clock energy `H_c`) therefore enters the mass that couples to motion and gravity, which is the substrate of mass-energy/proper-time clock coupling. Worked examples (hydrogenlike two-charge system on a post-Newtonian metric) show the prior "virial" couplings vanish under correct rest-frame coordinates.
