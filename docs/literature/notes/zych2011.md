## Core claim
A Mach–Zehnder interferometer carrying a particle with an evolving internal "clock" DOF in Earth's gravity loses interferometric visibility because proper time runs at different rates along the two arms, so the clock states become distinguishable and encode which-path information. Unlike the gravitational phase shift (always reinterpretable as an Aharonov–Bohm effect in flat spacetime), this visibility drop *cannot* be so reinterpreted and is therefore the first unambiguous witness of the genuinely general-relativistic notion of proper time in quantum mechanics.

## Method
Two-arm interferometer with internal clock states |τ_i⟩ on each path. Visibility V = |⟨τ₁|τ₂⟩| (Eq. 5), the clock-state overlap; complementarity gives V² + D² = 1 with D the which-path distinguishability. Internal energy couples to the gravitational potential via the effective Hamiltonian (Eq. 7), giving V = |cos(ΔE ΔV ΔT / 2ℏc²)| (Eq. 13) with ΔE the internal energy gap and ΔV = gΔh the potential difference. The proper-time difference is Δτ = ΔV ΔT / c² (Eq. 15); visibility recast via the Mandelstam–Tamm orthogonalization time t⊥ = πℏ/ΔE as V = |cos((Δτ/t⊥)(π/2))| (Eq. 16). ## Relation to the five Coastline claims
- **Claim I — emergent proper time / temporal record:** Supports operationally. The internal clock DOF is an explicit *physical* temporal record: its distinguishability D measures how much elapsed-proper-time information has been written, directly matching Claim I's "any DOF carrying usable info about elapsed evolution." Note the framing is single-particle/two-path rather than emergence-from-many-records; proper time here is a property *carried by* a DOF, which is the substrate Claim I generalizes, not yet the multipartite emergence.
- **Claim II — redundancy / stability / compressibility:** Largely silent — this is a single bipartite (clock vs path) record with no proliferation, redundancy plateau, or multiple-observer consensus. It supplies the *physics* of how a temporal record forms (proper-time difference → orthogonalizing clock states) but not the redundancy/stability/compressibility statistics Claim II needs. At most it gives the elementary "unit record" whose redundant copies a Claim-II account would have to count.
- **Claim III — failure mode / nuisance-discrimination:** Strongly supports the witness-vs-nuisance distinction — arguably its sharpest contribution. The paper explicitly separates the visibility drop (genuine proper-time effect, witness) from the gravitational phase shift (reinterpretable as AB nuisance, occurs whether or not the system is a clock). This is the structural-failure/nuisance discrimination of Claim III at the single-record level: only the visibility loss diagnoses that proper time has operational meaning. Table 1's "sharp DOF vs broadened (σ_τ) vs not-a-DOF" rows prefigure the framework's structural-failure spectrum.
- **Claim IV — operational anchors (Fisher / mutual info / cross-probe / coarse-graining):** Supports Fisher/time-energy and cross-probe anchors; silent on mutual-info-between-records and coarse-graining. The orthogonalization time t⊥ = πℏ/ΔE is a Mandelstam–Tamm (Fisher-information-like, time-energy) resource, and V = |cos((Δτ/t⊥)(π/2))| ties visibility to it cleanly. The visibility itself is a cross-probe-mismatch quantity (overlap of two clock readings on two arms). No multi-fragment mutual information; coarse-graining/robustness not analyzed.
- **Claim V — positioning (PW bipartite vs quantum-Darwinism configurational vs multipartite redundancy):** Sits at the *bipartite, single-record* pole — closer to Page–Wootters in spirit (a clock DOF correlated with a system/path, time as a relational/entanglement variable) than to multipartite redundancy. It does not instantiate the framework's multipartite-redundancy thesis; it is the relativistic, operational version of "proper time as a quantum DOF" that the multipartite framework must promote from a single record to a redundant consensus.
