## Core claim

Proposes **functional information** `F_QD(δ) = log₂ R_δ` as an *operational, threshold-grounded* measure of classical objectivity in Quantum Darwinism. Rather than asking how much total correlation an environment fragment shares with the system, it asks how many fragments *individually* clear an operationally meaningful adequacy bar — carrying at least `(1−δ)H_S` bits of **classically accessible** pointer information, where accessibility is ceilinged by the **Holevo quantity** (not the quantum mutual information). The redundancy `R_δ` is the abundance of such adequate fragments, extracted from *onset statistics* (the fragment size at which a typical fragment becomes adequate) rather than from an ad-hoc fit to a partial-information plot. Simulations of a heterogeneous pure-dephasing model exhibit three robust features: rapid early-time growth of `ln R_δ`, a smooth crossover, and a **capacity-limited plateau** `F_QD^plateau ≲ log₂ N` set by the environment's recording capacity (not by the stringency of `δ`). A Landauer-type argument gives a thermodynamic floor: each additional bit of `F_QD` *doubles* the minimal heat dissipation needed to stabilise the records, framing objectivity as a quantifiable, resource-limited phenomenon.

## Method

- **Adequacy predicate (Holevo-based).** A fragment `F` is *δ-adequate* when `χ(Π_S : F) ≥ (1−δ)H_S` (Eq. 6), where `χ` is the Holevo quantity for the system's pointer POVM `Π_S` and `H_S` the Shannon entropy of pointer outcomes. The bound is deliberately *one-sided/conservative*: it credits only classically accessible information, never mere correlation.
- **Onset statistics → redundancy.** For each fragment size `m`, the adequacy frequency `Φ_δ(m)` is the probability a random size-`m` fragment is adequate (nondecreasing in `m` by data-processing). The *onset* `m*_δ = min{ m : Φ_δ(m) ≥ ½ }` (median threshold); redundancy `R_δ ≈ N/m*_δ = 1/f_δ` (Eqs. 9–11), and `F_QD(δ) = log₂ R_δ` (Eq. 12). Each bit of `F_QD` ⇒ a doubling of `R_δ` / halving of the resource share `f_δ`.
- **Capacity bounds.** `χ ≤ min{H_S, log₂ d_F}` (Eq. 13) gives a capacity condition `log₂ d_F ≥ (1−δ)H_S` and a plateau `F_QD^plateau ≤ log₂ N` (Eq. 18).
- **Model.** System = qubit (pointer basis `{|0⟩,|1⟩}`); environment = `N` spin-½ subenvironments; pure-dephasing `H_SE = σ_z^{(S)} ⊗ Σ_k g λ_k σ_z^{(k)}` (Eq. 19) with *heterogeneous* couplings `λ_k` drawn i.i.d. exponential (mean 1) — disorder modelling realistic inhomogeneity. Pointer basis = `σ_z` eigenbasis (populations conserved; only coherences decay). Fragment-conditional overlap `c_F(t) = exp[−(gt)² Σ_{k∈F} λ_k]` (Eq. 22) — a **Gaussian** (disorder-/non-Markovian-flavoured) decay; Holevo reduces to `χ = h₂((1+|c_F|)/2)` (Eq. 24).
- **Statistics.** Random and disjoint fragment sampling (both agree); monotonicity enforced by isotonic regression; 95% CIs by nonparametric bootstrap; an overlap correction `R^eff` (Eq. 28) removes mid-time inflation from shared subenvironments under random sampling.
- **Numbers.** Redundancy trajectories show early-time `R_δ(t) ∼ e^{κt}` with fitted rates `κ_δ ∈ [3.35, 5.15]` bits/time (larger `δ` ⇒ faster onset), then saturation at `R_δ^plateau ≈ 1.5×10⁵` (`F_QD ≈ 17` bits `≈ log₂ N` for `N ≈ 1.3×10⁵`). Stricter tolerance shifts onset later but leaves the plateau unchanged. *(NB: §F states "`N = 64` unless otherwise stated," but the plateau figures use the large `N ≈ 1.3×10⁵`.)*

## Relation to the five Coastline claims

- **Claim I — emergent proper time / temporal record:** *Silent on time; configurational.* The recorded quantity is the system's static `σ_z` pointer, not elapsed evolution. The "functional"/adequacy framing (a fragment is adequate if it lets an observer *infer the pointer outcome with high confidence*) aligns with Claim I's inferential-readout stance, but instantiates Claim I's borrowed architecture, not its temporal specialisation. Same standing as the rest of the QD cluster.
- **Claim II — redundancy / stability / compressibility:** *The sharpest **configurational** redundancy instrument in the (now-extended) corpus.* `R_δ = N/m*_δ` and `F_QD = log₂ R_δ` operationalise the **redundancy** leg directly; the `F_QD^plateau ≲ log₂ N` ceiling is the compressibility/capacity statement; the early-growth-then-plateau trajectory is the redundancy-formation dynamics. **Crucially**, grounding adequacy in the *Holevo* bound (classically accessible information) rather than the quantum mutual information is an explicit guard against *overcounting* — the precise limitation the synthesis (§2) records for the MI anchor ("entanglement-enhanced cases give high `I` without redundancy"). NB configurational throughout.
- **Claim III — failure mode / nuisance-discrimination:** *Indirect but transferable.* The Holevo-vs-QMI distinction is a conservative discrimination principle (genuine objectivity-bearing records vs. mere correlation); the heterogeneous-coupling Gaussian overlap decay separates *coherence loss* from *record formation* in the spirit of [[riedel2011]] ("decoherence rate ≠ record-keeping rate"). Not a temporal nuisance-discrimination.
- **Claim IV — operational anchors:** *Sharpens the mutual-information anchor.* Argues the operational ceiling for redundancy counting is the **Holevo quantity** (classically accessible information), not total correlations — a direct input to the deferred Claim IV **measure-registration protocol** (which classification anchor to register). Silent on Fisher and cross-probe mismatch. Touches the coarse-graining/redundancy anchor via onset statistics.
- **Claim V — positioning:** *The configurational / quantum-Darwinism pole.* A multipartite-redundancy measure for *configurational* records — exactly the redundancy quantity the framework claims, but not yet temporal, and not Page–Wootters. Reinforces the QD pole of the Claim-V map.

- **Methodological note (temporal-redundancy functional, `docs/notes/`):** *Direct and load-bearing.* This is the operational configurational `F_QD = log₂ R_δ` measure that MN v0.2 transplants onto a temporal pointer; the definitions coincide (`R_δ = N/m*_δ` here ↔ `R_δ = N/m_δ` there; `H_S` ↔ `H_T`; Holevo `χ` ↔ the MN's `I(T:F)`). MN v0.2 already cites this preprint; this note is its corpus record.
- **Claim IV measure-registration / CL-2026-006:** candidate principle — register the *classically accessible* (Holevo) information as the classification anchor, not the QMI, to guard against the entanglement-enhanced overcounting flagged in synthesis §2 and MN §4.
- **Coastline deferred items:** the Landauer/thermodynamic floor (`F_QD` ↔ heat) is a concrete contact point for the deferred *arrow-of-time / thermodynamic* item.
