## Summary

A single-author conceptual preprint proposing that clock time is not fundamental but an emergent calibration of accumulated *physical distinguishability*. It introduces a "causal-informational" parameter Λ_F, defined as the Fisher-geometric arc length accumulated along a causally admissible trajectory in state space (classical Fisher information metric, or quantum Fisher information / Bures / Fubini–Study geometry in the quantum case). Ordinary time t is then recovered only after a stable reference process ("clock") provides a monotonic, reproducible calibration t_C = f_C(Λ_F^(C)). The paper is explicitly a reformulation/research-program proposal, not a new dynamical theory, and it candidly lists its own limitations.

## Key points

- Central hypothesis: "clock time is reconstructed from the Fisher distinguishability accumulated along causally ordered physical changes" — distinguishability is the primitive, time is a calibrated by-product. Stated analogy to temperature as an emergent statistical variable.
- Λ_F defined via the Fisher information line element dΛ_F² = g_ij^(F) dθ^i dθ^j (classical) and via the QFI / Bures / Fubini–Study metric (quantum); for unitary evolution dΛ_Q = (2ΔH/ħ)dt, so the construction reduces to the standard energy-spread / quantum-speed-limit (Mandelstam–Tamm) relations re-read as bounds on distinguishability rate.
- Explicitly distinguishes classical (measurement-dependent) Fisher information from measurement-optimized QFI, and stresses Λ_F is reparameterization-invariant (arc-length-like) but path- and model-dependent — "not an absolute cosmic clock."
- Five worked examples: classical oscillator phase clock, qubit clock (Λ_Q = ωt), exponential decay, "Fisher distinguishability per tick" η_C and a stability functional S_C as a clock-quality measure, and a cosmological-redshift / Fisher-matrix sketch.
- Positions itself relative to Page–Wootters relational time, Connes–Rovelli thermal time, quantum speed limits, information geometry (Ito), and the quantum-gravity problem of time, claiming the novelty is treating Fisher distinguishability as the *operational precursor* to clock time.

## Flags

Title and author confirmed (J. Sumaya-Martínez, UAEM). PDF complete (13 pp.).

Critical read (per instructions): The preprint is intellectually careful and notably self-aware, NOT crankish — it has an explicit Limitations section and an Originality Statement that disclaim novelty of "time is emergent," restricting the claim to Fisher distinguishability as precursor. The mathematics is correct but largely standard reparameterization: eq. (27) i∂_{Λ_Q}|ψ⟩ = Ĥ/(2ΔH)|ψ⟩ is just Schrödinger's equation rewritten with arc length as the parameter, and the author correctly notes it is conceptual rather than a new equation (and breaks when ΔH = 0). Genuine weaknesses to flag: (i) no novel falsifiable prediction — every example reproduces standard time-parameterized physics; (ii) Λ_F is admitted to be path- and model-dependent, undercutting any claim to fundamentality, so the "deeper parameterization" framing is weaker than it sounds; (iii) the cosmology / "universal photosphere" material (Example V, §13.3) is hand-waving with no derivation. References are real and appropriate (Page–Wootters, Connes–Rovelli, Braunstein–Caves, Mandelstam–Tamm, Ito). Net: a sound but modest conceptual contribution, speculative in scope and not yet a calculational theory — treat its claims as programmatic, not established.
