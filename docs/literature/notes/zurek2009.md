## Summary

The defining progress-article statement of quantum Darwinism. It reframes the environment not as a passive "trash can" into which coherence is traced away, but as a witness/communication channel that holds many redundant imprints of a system's preferred states. Because observers in practice intercept only tiny fragments of the environment (e.g. a few scattered photons), only states that proliferate copies of themselves — the einselected pointer states — can be found out, and this redundancy is what makes them objective. The paper also sketches how branching/copying yields apparent collapse and an envariance-based route to Born's rule.

## Key points

- Quantum mutual information I(S:F) = H_S + H_F − H_SF between system S and an environment fragment F, plotted vs fragment size f, is the central diagnostic; its rapid rise to a plateau at H_S signals redundancy.
- Redundancy R_δ = 1/f_δ = number of independent fragments each supplying (1−δ) of the classical information; large R implies objectivity.
- Only pointer (commuting) observables can be redundantly copied; the no-cloning theorem forces a "non-linear" symmetry breaking that singles out a preferred set and explains why complementary observables cannot be objective.
- Branching-state structure |ψ_SE⟩ = Σ ψ_k |s_k⟩|ε_k^(1)⟩…|ε_k^(N)⟩: a "bud" pointer state grows redundant offspring in many subenvironments; reading one long branch fragment reveals the whole branch.
- Mixed environments still build redundancy plateaus (more slowly, ~1−h per bit), so objectivity is robust to noise; photons are ideal carriers because they barely interact with each other.

## Relevance to the framework

The most direct template for Claim II: the framework lifts the I(S:F) mutual-information plateau and redundancy ratio R_δ from configurational records to *temporal* records, asserting a classical proper-time variable emerges precisely when temporal inference is redundant, stable, and compressible. The "only commuting/pointer observables get copied" result motivates the framework's claim that proper time is a special emergent variable, not an arbitrary one, and the mutual-information-between-records diagnostic is Claim IV almost verbatim.
