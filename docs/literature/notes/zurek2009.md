## Core claim
The environment is not a passive sink for coherence but a witness/communication channel holding many redundant imprints of a system's preferred (einselected pointer) states; because observers intercept only tiny fragments, only states that proliferate copies of themselves can be found out, and this redundancy is what makes them objective.

## Method
Quantum-Darwinism formalism. The central diagnostic is the quantum mutual information I(S:F) = H_S + H_F − H_SF between system S and an environment fragment F, plotted against fragment size f: a rapid rise to a plateau at H_S signals redundancy. Redundancy R_δ = 1/f_δ counts independent fragments each supplying (1−δ) of the classical information; large R implies objectivity. The no-cloning theorem forces only commuting/pointer observables to be redundantly copied. Branching states |ψ_SE⟩ = Σ ψ_k |s_k⟩|ε_k^(1)⟩…|ε_k^(N)⟩ describe a pointer "bud" growing redundant offspring across subenvironments; mixed environments still build (slower) redundancy plateaus, so objectivity is noise-robust.

## Relation to the five Coastline claims
- **Claim I — emergent proper time / temporal record:** Supports by analogy. Establishes that an objective classical property is whatever the environment proliferates copies of — an emergent, record-defined variable rather than a fundamental microscopic one. The framework reads each environment-fragment imprint as the configurational prototype of a "temporal record." Paper is about configurational properties and silent on time itself.
- **Claim II — redundancy / stability / compressibility:** Supports (most direct template). The three framework legs are all present and named: redundancy (R_δ, many independent copies), stability (einselected pointer/commuting observables survive), compressibility (the plateau means a small fragment already carries essentially all the classical information). The framework lifts this verbatim to: a classical proper-time variable emerges when temporal inference is redundant, stable, and compressible.
- **Claim III — failure mode / nuisance-discrimination:** Supports. The "only commuting/pointer observables get copied" result and the no-cloning-driven symmetry breaking explain *why* complementary observables cannot be objective — i.e., when no redundancy plateau forms, there is no consensus variable. This is the configurational template for discriminating structural failure (no plateau / no preferred set) from mere noise (slower-but-present plateau in mixed environments).
- **Claim IV — operational anchors (Fisher / mutual info / cross-probe / coarse-graining):** Strong anchor. I(S:F) is literally "mutual information between system and record," and its plateau-vs-fragment-size behavior is the robustness-under-coarse-graining anchor; R_δ is the redundancy anchor. The framework's "mutual information between temporal records" is essentially I(S:F) re-pointed at time. No Fisher information here (that anchor comes from [[smith2020]]); cross-probe mismatch implicit in fragment agreement.
- **Claim V — positioning (PW bipartite vs quantum-Darwinism configurational vs multipartite redundancy):** Supports as the defining *configurational* multipartite-redundancy pole. This is the paper the framework is most explicitly modeled on; the Coastline contribution is to keep the multipartite-redundancy structure but record elapsed time instead of configuration, distinguishing it from the PW bipartite-correlation pole ([[page1983]]).
