## Summary
Argues that the resource enabling the Page–Wootters (PaW) clock to function is the *internal coherence* of the global state, and constructs a measure for it. Using a two-qubit Bell-diagonal toy model with a Zeeman Hamiltonian and a dephasing operation that enforces the energy superselection rule, the authors show that the relative entropy of coherence splits into an external part (Holevo asymmetry, tied to reference-frame/speakable-information tasks) and an internal part that governs how well the clock-conditioned probabilities track elapsed time.

## Key points
- Splits total coherence: C_r(ρ) = C_r(D(ρ)) + A_G(ρ), internal + external. Internal coherence C_r(D(ρ)) = S(Δ(ρ)) − S(D(ρ)) is identified as the quantity responsible for PaW clock operation (Proposition 3.2).
- Probability of "agreement" between clock and system, Pr(R₁|R₂), correlates with internal coherence: it is 0.75 for fully dephased states, 0.5 (no time sense / no clock) when internal coherence vanishes, and 1 (perfect clock) at maximum.
- Key conceptual result: entanglement is *neither necessary nor sufficient* for the PaW clock — the maximally entangled |φ⁺⟩ gives worst agreement (0.5) while |ψ⁺⟩ gives best, and a c₃=0 non-entangled family still yields nonzero agreement.
- Connects internal coherence to extractable work: single-shot work from coherence W(ρ) = kT·C_r(D(ρ)), and discusses work-locking under energy-conserving thermal operations.
- Reviews Pegg's "totality of physical reality" construction and extends the coherence measure to qudits.

## Relevance to the framework
Directly relevant to **Claim II** (criteria for a usable temporal variable) and **Claim III** (failure mode): the internal-coherence measure is an order parameter for whether a PaW-type clock-conditioning supports a usable temporal variable at all — it is maximal for a good clock and zero when no sense of time exists. The decoupling of clock function from entanglement sharpens the **Claim V** positioning: the relevant resource is coherence/asymmetry structure, not bipartite entanglement per se. Touches **Claim IV** indirectly (a single-number information measure on the clock-conditioning), though it is bipartite and not redundancy-based.
