## The External Claim

Sorci et al. (2026) propose that in trapped optical ion clocks with strongly squeezed motional states, the unitary clock-motion evolution generated by the Hamiltonian

> Ĥ = Ĥ_c + ℏω(n̂ + 1/2) − (ℏω / 2mc²) Ĥ_c P̂²

produces an entanglement between internal clock states and motional states that manifests as a measurable reduction in Ramsey interferometric visibility:

> V ≃ 1 − (ε_m ω_c t)² sinh²(2r) / 16

For ²⁷Al⁺ parameters (ε_m = 3.3 × 10⁻¹⁸), a 20 MHz trap, t ≃ 1 s, and squeezing parameter r = 2.26, the predicted visibility reduction is V ≃ 0.93. The authors state that this would constitute the first experimental witness of clock-motion entanglement induced by quantum proper-time dynamics, in a regime where a classical proper-time parameter is no longer a sufficient description.

The claim has two components, separated here for ledger purposes:

- **C1 (sensitivity claim):** the predicted visibility loss is large enough to be measurable with state-of-the-art trapped-ion clock technology.
- **C2 (interpretive claim):** the observed visibility loss, if measured, would be a witness of quantum proper-time dynamics that cannot be reproduced by any semiclassical description of proper time.

The Ledger evaluates C2 against the Coastline. C1 is a sensitivity statement reviewable on its own technical merits; it is not the object of this entry.

## Declared Evaluating Measure

Per the Scout horizon signal on Claim IV measure indeterminacy, this Ledger entry declares its evaluating measure upfront:

> **Mutual information I(C : M) between the clock degree of freedom C and the motional degree of freedom M, evaluated on the reduced two-mode state after free evolution under the Sorci Hamiltonian.**

Justification of choice: Sorci's Eq. (12) visibility loss reflects the reduced clock coherence implied by clock-motion entanglement under unitary evolution. The visibility witness is therefore most naturally interpreted as evidence of nontrivial clock-motion correlation, for which mutual information provides a natural declared evaluating measure — the canonical information-theoretic quantity for shared structure between two records. Fisher information (Nambu-style, FIT-style) is the available alternative anchor; mutual information is selected here because the claim is structurally about *shared* temporal information between two records, which mutual information is the canonical measure for. The choice is registered; it does not foreclose later re-evaluation under another Claim-IV anchor in a subsequent Ledger entry.

## Classification Analysis

### Engagement with Coastline Claim II (Redundancy, Stability, Compressibility)

**Redundancy.** The Sorci protocol involves *two* temporal records: the clock internal state (carrying phase information at ω_c) and the motional state (carrying phase information at ω, modulated by the ε_m coupling). Under Coastline v0.2 terminology, the motional state qualifies as a temporal record because its state evolution carries inferentially usable information about elapsed evolution under the coupled Hamiltonian — specifically, the n̂-dependent frequency shift in Sorci's Eq. (5) makes the motional state a carrier of elapsed-time information accessible through the clock's reduced dynamics. This is the minimum nontrivial multipartite case — two carriers, not many. The Coastline's redundancy condition is satisfied in the weakest possible sense: there exist multiple carriers of temporal information. It is *not* satisfied in the stronger sense that would underwrite a robust classical τ_cl: there are not "many independent fragments each containing sufficient information" in the Zurek sense. **Status under Claim II.1: minimally engaged.**

**Stability.** Tracing out the motional degree of freedom yields the reduced clock state whose visibility *drops* under squeezing. Stability under partial trace is therefore explicitly *failing* in the predicted regime — this is the content of the visibility-loss result. **Status under Claim II.2: failing under the predicted regime, which is exactly the framework's prediction for the failure mode.**

**Compressibility.** The Sorci frequency-shift signatures (vSODS, sqSODS) are explicitly stated by the authors to be reproducible by a semiclassical ⟨τ⟩ description — they are compressible to a single coarse-grained parameter without inferential loss. The visibility loss is *not* so reproducible: no single τ_cl can simultaneously organise the phase evolution of clock and motional observables consistently in the squeezed regime. **Status under Claim II.3: failing for visibility, satisfied for frequency. The asymmetry is exactly the framework's prediction.**

### Verdict on the structural claim

The Sorci visibility-loss signal, structurally, is *consistent with* a partial failure of Coastline Claim II — specifically, a failure of stability (II.2) and compressibility (II.3) for the clock subsystem under partial trace of the motional record, in a regime where redundancy (II.1) is minimally engaged.

This is compatible with C2 in the *interpretive* sense: visibility loss would be a witness of structural failure of the conditions under which a classical proper-time variable emerges for the reduced clock subsystem. **However**, this does not yet classify C2 as COMPATIBLE with the framework, because the framework requires (Claim III, final paragraph) that *structural consensus failure be discriminable from generic nuisance-induced coherence loss*.

### Discriminability gap (load-bearing for classification)

The Sorci proposal does not provide a separability analysis distinguishing the predicted visibility loss from:

1. Motional heating ṅ during the t ≃ 1 s free-evolution window.
2. Motional dephasing γ_φ acting on the squeezed state.
3. Squeezing preparation infidelity (especially r-dependent).
4. Drive-phase noise during Ramsey interrogation.
5. Anomalous mode-mixing in multi-ion clock configurations (²⁷Al⁺ logic-ion architecture).
6. Detection infidelity correlated with motional state.

Each of these nuisance channels also reduces clock-motion mutual information by reducing the coherent two-mode structure that produces the predicted entanglement. Under the declared evaluating measure I(C : M), a generic decoherence channel that destroys the squeezed state's quantum coherence reduces I(C : M) just as effectively as the time-dilation Hamiltonian does. The two contributions are not separated by Sorci et al.'s analysis.

The Coastline's Weak Falsification clause (v0.2 §Falsifiability) requires that any proposed witness be *discriminable* from such channels by a separability analysis grounded in the operational measures of Claim IV. This requirement is not met by the Sorci proposal as published.

## Classification: **UNDERDETERMINED**

The Sorci et al. claim C2 is classified **UNDERDETERMINED** against Coastline v0.2 under the declared evaluating measure I(C : M).

**The Hamiltonian-level structural claim is framework-compatible. The published evidentiary package is framework-underdetermined.** The classification weighs against the present sufficiency of the proposal as a witness, not against the conceptual programme it advances. The structural alignment is real: the predicted visibility loss is, at the level of Hamiltonian dynamics, a partial failure of the framework's Claim-II conditions in exactly the predicted pattern. The interpretive elevation to "witness of quantum proper-time dynamics" requires discriminability against generic nuisance channels that is not present in the published proposal.

The classification can move to COMPATIBLE on satisfaction of the discriminant conditions below.

## Discriminant Conditions

The classification UNDERDETERMINED may be upgraded to **COMPATIBLE** on demonstration of all of the following:

**D1.** An explicit nuisance-channel decomposition of the I(C : M) loss in the squeezed-state Ramsey protocol, identifying the contributions from (i) time-dilation Hamiltonian unitary entanglement, (ii) motional heating, (iii) motional dephasing, (iv) squeezing preparation infidelity, and (v) detection-side correlated infidelity.

**D2.** A null-test protocol in which the predicted time-dilation contribution to I(C : M) is suppressed (e.g. by reducing ε_m via lower trap frequency, heavier ion, or lower clock frequency) while the nuisance channels are not, *provided that any associated change in the nuisance channels under the parameter shift is independently bounded or modelled*. Observed I(C : M) loss in the null configuration then places a bound on the nuisance budget that must be subtracted from the signal configuration; the soundness of the null test depends on the independence assumption being verified, not merely stipulated.

**D3.** A measured r-dependence of the visibility loss consistent with sinh²(2r) scaling (Sorci Eq. 12), distinguished from r-dependence consistent with squeezing-prep infidelity scaling.

The classification may be downgraded to **INCONSISTENT** on demonstration that:

**D−1.** I(C : M) loss observed in the predicted regime is fully accounted for by the nuisance budget bounded in D2, with no statistically significant residual attributable to time-dilation Hamiltonian unitary entanglement.

The classification may also be upgraded to COMPATIBLE under a *different* declared evaluating measure in a separate Ledger entry. The selection of I(C : M) here does not foreclose Fisher-information-anchored or cross-probe-mismatch-anchored re-evaluations.
