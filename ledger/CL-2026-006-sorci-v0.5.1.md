## The External Claim

Sorci et al. (2026) propose that in trapped optical ion clocks with strongly squeezed motional states, the unitary clock-motion evolution generated by the Hamiltonian

> Ĥ = Ĥ_c + ℏω(n̂ + 1/2) − (ℏω / 2mc²) Ĥ_c P̂²

produces an entanglement between internal clock states and motional states that manifests as a measurable reduction in Ramsey interferometric visibility:

> V ≃ 1 − (ε_m ω_c t)² sinh²(2r) / 16

For ²⁷Al⁺ parameters (ε_m = 3.3 × 10⁻¹⁸), a 20 MHz trap, t ≃ 1 s, and squeezing parameter r = 2.26, the predicted visibility reduction is V ≃ 0.93. The authors state that this would constitute the first experimental witness of clock-motion entanglement induced by quantum proper-time dynamics, in a regime where a classical proper-time parameter is no longer a sufficient description.

The claim has two components, separated here for ledger purposes:

- **C1 (sensitivity claim):** the predicted visibility loss is large enough to be measurable with state-of-the-art trapped-ion clock technology.
- **C2 (interpretive claim):** the observed visibility loss, if measured, would be a witness of quantum proper-time dynamics that cannot be reproduced by any semiclassical description of proper time.

The Ledger evaluates C2 against the Coastline. C1 is a sensitivity statement reviewable on its own technical merits; it is not the object of this entry.

### Engagement under the R_δ (temporal-redundancy) anchor

Read through R_δ, the Sorci protocol is the **minimum-multipartite (N = 2) anti-redundant case** (MN v0.3 §3): squeezing drives the reduced clock toward the anti-redundant pole (R_δ ↓ as V ↓), the same Claim II.2 stability failure the I(C : M) reading records. At N = 2, R_δ ∈ {undefined, 1, 2} — a structural/directional reading, **not** a quantitative R_δ(r) curve.

**v0.5 — the N ≫ 2 forward target now has a candidate exemplar.** The R_δ-specific quantitative discriminant (a measured `R_δ(r) ∼ sinh²(2r)` curve, or its redundant-record analogue) requires the many-carrier setting, which the toolkit's Module 3b now realises numerically as `R_δ(N, γ_φ t)` curves for an open temporal instrument. This is registered as a *candidate worked exemplar* for Coastline v0.4's principal open problem, carried under the following caveat (verbatim, propagated from the Module 3b contract §4 and MN v0.2 §1.1):

> **Einselection caveat.** Module 3b computes `R_δ(N, γ_φ, t)` *conditional on the assumption that the temporal pointer `T` is einselected* (MN v0.2 §1.1). The existence of a dynamically-derived temporal-pointer basis — the temporal analogue of environment-induced superselection — is the hardest part of the Coastline's principal open problem (**Anti-Claim #6**) and is **not** addressed: it is *assumed*, not derived. Module 3b is therefore a **candidate** worked exemplar of a *redundant temporal record* **within the assumed regime**, not a resolution of Anti-Claim #6, which **remains open**. Whether any such instance counts as *the* worked exemplar that converts Coastline Claim II from analogy to demonstration is a Coastline-level judgement reserved to the steward (Lock-Key).

Module 3b is a property of the *open-temporal-instrument* construction, not of the N = 2 Sorci protocol; it bears on this entry only as the realisation of the R_δ-anchor forward target, and adds **no** new discriminant on the two-carrier Sorci claim.

## v0.5 — Quantitative grounding of Discriminant D1 (tmc-numerics Module 3a)

The toolkit produces the per-channel decomposition D1 names, under a four-layer nuisance model (initial-state / dynamical-Lindblad / stochastic-Hamiltonian / read-out), in the rotating-frame secular-dispersive model that reproduces Sorci Eq. (12) (the derived coupling `g_md = −ε_m ω_c/4` validated to the Eq.-(12) coefficient; ²⁷Al⁺ extrapolation `V ≈ 0.943`, matching the published `V ≃ 0.93`). The budget (committed: `numerics/results/sorci_nuisance_budget_v0.1.json`) decomposes the I(C : M) and visibility response into marginal-from-signal per-channel contributions, with the full model and the interaction residual reported.

**Three findings sharpen — rather than close — the discriminability gap:**

1. **The per-channel signs are channel- and regime-specific, not uniform.** Heating and motional dephasing **reduce** I(C : M) (ΔI ≈ −0.021, −0.034 bits at the scaled operating point), as a generic decoherence channel does; whereas the modelled squeezing-preparation infidelity **raises** I(C : M) (ΔI ≈ +0.16) and dominates the budget (symmetric depolarisation spreads population across Fock levels, increasing the mixed-state clock–motion correlations). A naïve "any nuisance lowers the shared-structure measure" intuition is therefore false — the discriminant cannot rest on the *sign* or *magnitude* of a single channel's I(C : M) contribution, only on its **controlled scaling** (exactly what D2/D3 demand).

2. **The detection layer is latent-invariant.** Detection-correlated infidelity leaves I(C : M) and the latent clock coherence **exactly unchanged** (ΔI = ΔV_state = 0); it degrades only the *observed* visibility (Δ(1−V_obs) ≈ 0.10 at ε_det = 0.05). D1's "detection-side correlated infidelity" contribution is thus an **observed-readout** effect, structurally distinct from the dynamical channels — a distinction the budget makes explicit and any experimental D1 must respect.

3. **The decomposition is model-conditional (Anti-Claim A4), and the budget exposes two model sensitivities** that an experimental D1 must pin down: (i) motional dephasing is **visibility-invisible in the secular dispersive model** — `D[n̂]` leaves the Fock populations, hence the dispersive clock coherence `V_state`, unchanged (ΔV_state ≈ 0) — **yet it still lowers quantum `I(C:M)`** (ΔI ≈ −0.034, by destroying the motional Fock coherences), and it would additionally shift `V_state` in the full `σ_z P̂²` model; (ii) the v0.1 symmetric-depolarising preparation-infidelity model is **aggressive for a high-cutoff bosonic mode** (it spreads population across all Fock levels), which is why it dominates here; a physically faithful squeezing-prep model (r-jitter / thermal admixture) is a registered toolkit-v0.2 refinement. The interaction residual (ΔI ≈ −0.021) confirms the channels are **non-additive** at finite nuisance.

**Bearing on the discriminant.** D1's *theoretical* component — "an explicit nuisance-channel decomposition of the I(C : M) loss" — is now **produced and reproducible** (committed artifact + notebook). But the budget shows that the decomposition's per-channel attribution is **model-conditional**, so the toolkit *sharpens* D1 into a demand on controlled scaling and explicit channel models, rather than supplying a model-free attribution. D2 (null test) and D3 (measured r-scan) remain **experimental and unmet**. The classification therefore **stays UNDERDETERMINED**; the toolkit grounds D1 and sharpens D1–D3, it does not discharge them.

## Discriminant Conditions

**D1.** An explicit nuisance-channel decomposition of the I(C : M) loss in the squeezed-state Ramsey protocol, identifying the contributions from (i) time-dilation Hamiltonian unitary entanglement, (ii) motional heating, (iii) motional dephasing, (iv) squeezing preparation infidelity, and (v) detection-side correlated infidelity. **v0.5:** the *theoretical/model* decomposition is now produced by tmc-numerics Module 3a (committed budget; per-channel marginals + full + residual; latent/observed split). D1 is satisfied as an *upgrade condition* only when this decomposition is **matched to experimental data** under controlled scaling — i.e. jointly with D2/D3 — not by the theoretical budget alone.

**D2.** *(Experimental, unchanged.)* A null-test protocol suppressing the time-dilation contribution while nuisance channels are not, with co-variation independently bounded.

**D3.** *(Experimental, unchanged.)* A measured r-dependence of the visibility loss consistent with sinh²(2r) scaling, distinguished from squeezing-prep-infidelity scaling. *(The toolkit confirms the clean sinh²(2r) signal in the zero-nuisance limit and that the depolarising prep-infidelity model scales differently — supporting D3's premise that the two are separable in principle.)*

**D−1 (downgrade to INCONSISTENT).** *(Unchanged.)* I(C : M) loss fully accounted for by the D2 nuisance budget with no significant time-dilation residual.

The R_δ anchor adds no new discriminant at N = 2; its quantitative discriminant requires N ≫ 2 and is registered as a forward target (now with the Module 3b candidate exemplar, under the einselection caveat above).
